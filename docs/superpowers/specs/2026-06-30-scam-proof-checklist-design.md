# Design Spec: The Scam-Proof Checklist Lead Magnet
**Date:** 2026-06-30
**Brand:** Curious & Capable
**Goal:** Grow email list by offering a high-urgency, instantly useful PDF to adults 60+

---

## Purpose

A free PDF lead magnet that addresses Margaret's highest-urgency fear: not being able to tell a scam from a real message, call, or pop-up. Delivered via email opt-in. Drives list growth from Facebook, Substack, and in-person workshops.

---

## The PDF: "The Scam-Proof Checklist"

**Full title:** The Scam-Proof Checklist: 5 Warning Signs and What to Do When You're Not Sure

### Format and Design
- Single page, printable
- Minimum 14pt body font for readability
- Curious & Capable logo and brand colors at the top
- Two columns or stacked sections (whichever prints cleaner)
- Heavy white space throughout — no dense paragraphs
- Designed to be printed and kept by the phone or computer, not saved in a downloads folder
- Footer: "From Michael McIntosh at Curious & Capable. More at [website URL]"

---

### Section 1: Know the Signs

Five plain-language warning signs, written in Margaret's own language. No security jargon.

1. They're pushing you to act RIGHT NOW and won't let you think it over
2. They're asking for a gift card, wire transfer, or unusual payment method
3. A pop-up appeared saying your computer is infected and you need to call a number
4. Someone claiming to be your bank is asking for your account number or password
5. Something feels off, even if you can't explain why — trust that feeling

Each sign is a single, plain sentence. No bullet sub-points. No explanations longer than one line.

---

### Section 2: When You're Not Sure, Do This

A four-step protocol Margaret can follow in the moment when something feels wrong.

1. Stop. Don't click anything, don't call any number on the screen, don't give any information.
2. Close the window or hang up the phone. You can always call back on a number you already know.
3. Call someone you trust before you do anything else — a family member, a friend, anyone.
4. Report it at reportfraud.ftc.gov or by calling 1-877-382-4357. Getting scammed is not your fault. It happens to careful, intelligent people every day. You don't have to be embarrassed, and you don't have to handle it alone.

The final note in step 4 ("Getting scammed is not your fault...") is load-bearing copy. It addresses the shame that keeps Margaret from reporting and from asking for help. Do not cut it.

## Success Criteria

- Margaret opts in without needing to ask what she's getting
- She prints it, reads it in under two minutes, and keeps it somewhere she'll see it again
- The checklist models the voice and patience of the full Curious & Capable program
- Each new subscriber is warmed toward the 8-week program

---

## What This Is Not

This checklist does not try to teach comprehensive scam literacy. It solves one problem: what Margaret does in the moment when something feels wrong. Scope is intentionally narrow. Everything else belongs in the program.
