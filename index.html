    Why movement breaks matter

    What happens in the next 60 minutes

    • Circulation drops 50% — blood pools in your lower legs within one hour of sitting
    • Metabolism slows — your body enters an energy-saving mode and calorie burning drops significantly
    • Fat-burning enzyme shuts off — lipoprotein lipase, which breaks down fats in your bloodstream, deactivates
    • Insulin resistance begins — muscles stop contracting, reducing their ability to clear glucose from your blood
    • Spinal compression increases — lumbar disc pressure is higher when seated than when standing or lying down

    What accumulates over months and years

    Cardiovascular

    • 54% higher heart attack risk
    • Stiffened arteries
    • Plaque buildup

    Metabolic

    • Chronic high blood pressure
    • Midsection weight gain
    • Type 2 diabetes

    Musculoskeletal

    • Gluteal muscle wasting
    • Bone mineral loss
    • Early spinal disc degeneration

    Vascular

    • Varicose veins
    • Life-threatening Deep Vein Thrombosis blood clots

    Why your gym session is not enough

    Medical research shows that standard workouts at the end of the day cannot completely undo the damage of sitting all day. The damage happens in real time, hour by hour.

    The solution is frequent micro-movement: stand up and move for just 2 to 5 minutes for every 30 to 60 minutes you spend seated. One minute of bodyweight movement restarts your metabolism and muscle glucose uptake immediately.
